# Sign-Off Gate + the Split-Award Correction

**Date:** 2026-06-17
**Source:** `20260528_P&O_Leadership_Sign-Off.pptx` (16 slides), the real leadership sign-off deck for the 2026 P&O cycle.

This session covers the second governance gate (Phase 6, Sign-Off) and a foundational correction to the award grain that only surfaced here.

---

## The headline: split awards are real

The recommendation tables award a single DC to **multiple suppliers**: "Onions52, Owyhee", "Keystone, Onions52, Owyhee", "Gumz, Legacy Fresh", "Eagle Eye, Owyhee". A DC × lot × timeframe cell is **not** won by one supplier. Volume is **allocated across one or more suppliers**, capacity-constrained, with the human deciding the split.

**This breaks the old spec's locked rule** ("one supplier wins one DC × lot × timeframe cell", Appendix B non-negotiable #1, called "locked and everything downstream depends on it"). It is wrong.

**Why it matters / what it reframes:**
- The models are called **Allocation** models for a reason. The engine allocates volume across suppliers per cell; it does not pick a single winner.
- Capacity is load-bearing: no single supplier absorbs a whole cell, so capacity per supplier per scope constrains the split.
- The scenario tool sums per-supplier all-in costs because volume is divided.
- The kickoff "how volumes get divided" decision *is* the allocation.
- **Award grain corrected:** the cell is DC × lot × timeframe; the award is a **set of supplier shares** at that cell, not a single supplier. Each share carries its own volume and price.

This is the most important structural correction in the intake, larger than period grain. It resolves the Session 1 open question (split awards) definitively: yes, routinely.

---

## What the sign-off deck is

The leadership sign-off gate, made concrete. It is hand-built today and should be **generated from the selected scenario**. Four parts:

1. **Strategy recap** — the "why" carried forward from kickoff (e.g. cross-commodity leverage, longer-term contracts, consolidating bids into one with two timeframes).
2. **Recommendation per category** — per DC: Incumbent Supplier → Recommended Supplier(s), with R4 Savings vs STLY and +/- vs Incumbents. Split Conventional / Organic.
3. **Financial impact** — savings vs STLY, rolled to a portfolio total ($5.0M across categories; per category: Onions $0.2M, Colored Potatoes $0.6M, Russets $7.0M, Sweet Potatoes ($2.8M)).
4. **Supplier scorecard / share** — the sign-off scorecard snapshot (share, fill rate, on-time, volume).

**Portfolio-level.** One sign-off covers many categories (Onions, Colored Potatoes, Russets, Sweet Potatoes, and more) in a single leadership meeting. The gate aggregates many category allocation models into one approval.

---

## What it confirms

- **STLY is the headline metric.** "Savings vs STLY" is on every slide and is the number leadership approves. This is the payoff of the loaded fiscal calendar — STLY maps this cycle's periods to the same periods last year.
- **Conventional / Organic split** runs through every recommendation. The organic attribute from normalization drives the financial split; it is not cosmetic.
- **Sign-off scorecard = second freeze.** Supplier share, fill rate, on-time, volume captured at sign-off — the second of the two snapshots identified in Session 2.
- **Data lineage, printed:** "iTrade, CBS, Kroger P&O 2026 RFP responses." iTrade (cost/history), CBS (freight), RFP responses (bids). The exact feeds mapped in Session 3.
- **R4.** This cycle ran four rounds. Rounds are variable; sign-off lands after the last one.

---

## Build implications

- **Award model:** replace single-winner-per-cell with **supplier-share allocation per cell**. Each cell holds N awarded suppliers, each with volume share and price. Capacity constrains the allocation.
- **Sign-off output:** generated from the selected scenario — strategy recap + per-DC recommendation (incumbent → recommended, savings vs STLY, +/- vs incumbent, conv/organic) + portfolio financial roll-up + sign-off scorecard. Same generate-from-record pattern as the booking guide.
- **The gate is portfolio-level:** the sign-off spans multiple category cycles approved together, so the system needs a portfolio view above the single-category model.
